# Business Model: Ember Horizon Studios

**Model Type:** Self-Funded / Premium / Solo-Operated

**Objective:** To develop high-quality, "hook-driven" premium titles while maintaining financial independence through external employment.

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The studio focuses on the **Premium Indie** market, producing "narrow and deep" gaming experiences. By utilizing a full-time salary to cover all development and living expenses, the studio operates with zero debt and 100% equity retention. Success is measured by long-term community growth and "long-tail" sales on platforms like Steam and consoles.

## 2. Studio Identity & Legal "Firewall"

_To protect your assets and your day job, a clear separation is mandatory._

- **Legal Entity:** Establish a Single-Member LLC. This separates your personal liability from the studio's actions.
- **Financial Separation:** Maintain a dedicated business bank account. All "self-funding" should be transferred from your personal account to the business account as "Owner’s Investment."

## 3. Product Strategy: The Premium Model

The studio will avoid "Race to the Bottom" pricing (Free-to-Play/Ads) and focus on $15–$30 price points. Sub-$10 short-form horror titles (in the *Buckshot Roulette* / *Iron Lung* lineage) are also in scope when the concept calls for it — these can act as audience-building releases that lead into a fuller-priced title.

- **Genre Lane:** **Roguelite horror and psychological horror.** Horror is the constant; the mechanical chassis (deckbuilder, sim, deduction, rhythm, etc.) is selected per concept. Reference points: *Buckshot Roulette*, *Inscryption*, *Faith: The Unholy Trinity*, *Mouthwashing*, *Clover Pit*, *Crow Country*. See `studio_design_manifesto.md` for design pillars and `marketing/strategy.md` for audience.

- **The "Hook" Focus:** Solo games succeed when they have a "marketable hook"—a unique visual style or a specific mechanic that looks great in a 5-second GIF. In the horror lane, the hook is usually the *moment of dread* — a single frame or sound that telegraphs the entire premise.
    
- **Platform Priority:** **Steam First.** Steam’s algorithm is the most friendly to solo developers who can drive their own traffic. itch.io secondary for short-form horror titles — the audience there is dense and active.
    
- **Scope Management:** Use the **"Vertical Slice"** approach. Build one perfect level/loop first to prove the concept before expanding.
    

## 4. Operational Plan: The "Low-Friction" Workflow

_Since your time is limited, the business operates on "Sustainable Momentum" rather than "Crunched Cycles."_

- **The "Minimum Effective Dose":** Target **10–15 hours per week**. Consistency (1 hour every night) is more valuable for a solo dev than a 10-hour Saturday that leads to burnout.
    
- **Outsourcing Strategy:** Self-funding allows you to buy time. Instead of learning 3D modeling for six months, use the day job income to hire contractors for specific assets (Music, Key Art, UI) via marketplaces like ArtStation or specialized indie contractors.
    
- **Tech Stack:** Use "off-the-shelf" engines (Unity, Unreal, Godot) to maximize development speed. Avoid custom engines.
    

## 5. Financial Plan (Bootstrapping)

- **Revenue Reinvestment:** 100% of early game profits are reinvested into the studio (better tools, marketing, or legal fees).
    
- **Zero-Cost Marketing:** Focus on organic growth via:
    
    - **Devlogs:** Building a "Build in Public" presence on YouTube/X.
        
    - **Steam Festivals:** Participating in Steam Next Fest is the single most important "sales" event.
        
- **The "Quit" Trigger:** Clearly define the financial milestone (e.g., "12 months of salary in savings from game revenue") before considering leaving the day job.
    

## 6. Marketing & Community

- **Community as a Moat:** Build a Discord early. For a solo dev, 1,000 "true fans" who provide feedback and word-of-mouth are more effective than a $10k ad spend.
    
- **Wishlist Goal:** Aim for **7,000–10,000 Steam Wishlists** before launching to ensure the Steam algorithm picks you up on "Popular Upcoming."
    

## 7. Risk Management

|Risk|Mitigation Strategy|
|---|---|
|**Burnout**|Mandatory "no-code" days; focus on hobbies outside of tech.|
|**Scope Creep**|Use a "Kill List"—features that are cool but not essential to the core hook.|
|**Market Shift**|Constantly monitor Steam "Top Sellers" in your genre to ensure your "Hook" remains relevant.|

---

> **Pro-Tip:** In the 2026 market, players value **authenticity**. Use your "Solo Dev" status as a marketing asset. People love rooting for an individual creator more than a faceless corporation.

---

## 8. Genre Decision (resolved 2026-05-09)

**Working genre:** Roguelite horror / psychological horror.

**Why this lane:** The horror audience is durable, content-hungry, and rewards distinctive solo voices. Short-loop horror titles (*Buckshot Roulette*, *Iron Lung*, *Clover Pit*) have demonstrated that a tightly-scoped solo project can chart on Steam without enterprise-scale marketing. Pairing horror with a roguelite chassis lets a small content footprint feel large via run variety, and lets the mechanic itself carry the dread (the studio's design pillar #2: "The Mechanic Is the Meaning").

**Constraint:** Horror is the constant; the mechanical chassis is negotiable per concept. A concept must still pass all four manifesto pillars before being shortlisted. See the `concepts/` folder and the Game Ideas Notion DB for the active triage list.
