 **10–15 slide template** for a game pitch deck.

This template is designed to help solo developers transition from a high-level concept to a format that secures funding or interest from investors and publishers.

---

## **The Solo Indie Pitch Deck Template**

### **Slide 1: Title & Hook**

- **Game Title:** (The working title or final name).
    
- **Elevator Pitch:** A 1–2 sentence summary encapsulating the game’s essence.
    
- **Visual:** A single high-impact piece of key art that defines the "vibe".
    

### **Slide 2: The Design Pillars**

- **Core Principles:** List the 3–5 fundamental principles that define the experience.
    
- **The "North Star":** Briefly explain how these pillars act as the arbiter for all creative decisions.
    

### **Slide 3: High-Level Vision & Genre**

- **Genre/Platform:** Specify the genre (e.g., Action RPG) and target platforms (e.g., PC/Console).
    
- **Unique Selling Points (USPs):** What makes this game different from competitors?
    

### **Slide 4: The Core Gameplay Loop**

- **Action Sequence:** A diagram or description of the primary actions (e.g., Explore $\rightarrow$ Fight $\rightarrow$ Loot).
    
- **Psychological Hook:** Explain the rhythm of engagement and why it keeps players coming back.
    

### **Slide 5: Key Mechanics & Player Agency**

- **Movement & Combat:** Highlight unique player mechanics (e.g., "Fluid and Weighty Control").
    
- **Progression:** Briefly mention XP tables, skill trees, or power-up logic.
    

### **Slide 6: World, Narrative & Lore**

- **The Setting:** A summary of the environmental lore and history.
    
- **Character Profiles:** Visual references and brief descriptions of the protagonist or key NPCs.
    

### **Slide 7: Aesthetic Direction (The Moodboard)**

- **Visual Style:** Reference established media (e.g., "The lighting of _Blade Runner_ with the palette of _Hyper Light Drifter_").
    
- **Consistency:** Show how AI tools or seeds are used to maintain visual rhythm.
    

### **Slide 8: Target Audience & Market Analysis**

- **Player Profile:** Who is the intended audience?
    
- **Market Gap:** Why is now the right time for this specific game?
    

### **Slide 9: Technical Stack & AI Pipeline**

- **Engine & Middleware:** (e.g., Unity 6, Unreal Engine 5.5, FMOD).
    
- **AI Integration:** How you are using AI agents (e.g., context engineering or automated technical workflows) to scale production as a solo dev.
    

### **Slide 10: The "Tiny Playable Slice" (Prototype)**

- **Status:** Current state of the prototype and what has been validated.
    
- **Feedback:** Key takeaways from early interaction or playtesting.
    

### **Slide 11: Production Roadmap**

- **Timeline:** High-level milestones from MVP to commercialization.
    
- **Scope Management:** Mention "Quality Tiers" (Gold/Silver/Bronze) to show disciplined resource allocation.
    

### **Slide 12: The Business Case**

- **Monetization:** How the game will generate revenue.
    
- **Marketing Strategy:** Plan for promotional assets and community building.
    

### **Slide 13: The Team / The Creator**

- **Background:** Your experience and technical/creative strengths.
    
- **The "Director's Vision":** Why you are the right person to lead this "AI-powered orchestra".
    

### **Slide 14: The Ask**

- **Funding/Support:** Specifics on what you need from the stakeholder (funding, marketing, distribution).
    

### **Slide 15: Contact & Socials**

- Links to the GDD, devlogs, or portfolio.
