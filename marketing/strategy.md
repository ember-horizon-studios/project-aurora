# Marketing Strategy — Ember Horizon Studios

*Reference this file before starting any marketing task. Last updated: 2026-05-09.*

---

## Tone of Voice

**Serious. Confident. No bravado.**

Ember Horizon communicates with quiet conviction — not hype, not self-deprecating humor, not corporate polish. The studio has something to say and trusts the audience to receive it. Write like someone who believes deeply in what they're making, not like someone selling it.

- Do: Direct, intentional, grounded
- Don't: Hyperbolic ("AMAZING update!!!"), overly casual, or performatively humble
- Reference point: The way a thoughtful solo dev talks about their work when they're genuinely proud of it

---

## Target Audience

**Players who take interactivity seriously — and who like the lights low.**

Not a demographic — a mindset. These are players who come to games for the thinking, the discovery, and the moments that stick. They finish games (or runs) slowly because they're paying attention. They talk about games the way others talk about films.

Anchor references for the studio's current lane (roguelite + psychological horror):
- They've played and recommended *Buckshot Roulette*, *Inscryption*, *Faith: The Unholy Trinity*, *Mouthwashing*, *Crow Country*, *Iron Lung*.
- They'll also have played *Outer Wilds*, *Return of the Obra Dinn*, *Disco Elysium* — slow-comprehension games whose audience overlaps heavily with the horror-curious.
- They follow horror-leaning indie devs on X / YouTube. They watch dev streams. They wishlist obscure things and wait.

Secondary audience: the gamedev community (developers, journalists, publishers) — relevant for networking, press, and industry credibility, but not the primary content target.

---

## Content Pillars

Three pillars. Every post should fit at least one.

### 1. The Work
Behind-the-scenes development — decisions made, problems solved, things built. Shows intentionality in action. Appropriate for devlogs, short clips of something working, design decisions explained.

*Example: "Here's why I rebuilt the input system from scratch."*

### 2. The Experience
The feeling of playing — what the game evokes, what moments it creates. Connects the studio's philosophy to the player's world. Appropriate for mood posts, short gameplay captures, reflective writing.

*Example: A 15-second clip of a moment in the game that just feels right, no explanation needed.*

### 3. The Journey
The honest reality of building a studio alone — progress, setbacks, milestones. Humanizes the work without being self-indulgent. Appropriate for milestone posts, honest reflections, studio updates.

*Example: "P1 closed today. LLC registered, bank account open. One step closer."*

## Channel Strategy

| Channel | Role | Content Type | Cadence (target) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **X (@EmberHznStudios)** | Primary — community + industry | Text, images, short clips | 3–5x per week |
| **YouTube Shorts (@EmberHznStudios)** | Player reach + wishlist pipeline | Short video (native upload) | 1–2x per week when in active dev |
| **TikTok (@emberhznstudios)** | Player reach + discovery | Short video (same as YT Shorts, raw file) | Same as YouTube Shorts |

*Instagram, Threads, Bluesky, Twitch, Reddit handles reserved — activate when relevant.*

---

## Wishlist Strategy

Wishlists are a primary success metric on Steam. Content should funnel toward wishlist intent — not just engagement. Video content on YouTube Shorts and TikTok converts better to wishlists than text posts. When a game is announced, every piece of content should have a clear path to the Steam page.

---

## Rules

- Check this file before drafting any marketing content
- One canonical voice — don't let the tone drift between platforms
- Traction over polish: a real post today beats a perfect post next week
- Never announce without something to show
