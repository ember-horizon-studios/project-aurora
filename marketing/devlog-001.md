I started Ember Horizon Studios because I wanted more Agency in my life.

I want to be making games that make people think. Not just for the dopamine hits but for intrigue and philosophy. Horror and psychological horror themes are effective at this I feel but I don't want to limit my scope to just making games that are scary.

I want to be very intentional with the way I make games. I am inherently a very analytical person. Games with be thought out from the beginnings of market research to every implemented mechanic. I don't want to blindly go through development just because I have a good idea.

Right now, I am in early process of building out this game studio. Beyond a lot of administrative tasks like business, legal, and financial, I am currently in the process of triage-testing a handful of game concepts right now - the one that survives will be the debut title.
