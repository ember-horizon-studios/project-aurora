### Where do game ideas come from?

- Other Games
	- use existing games as jumping off points
	- change a games perspective, world, theme, context, medium
	- make a better version of a game
	- a game will change when fed through your unique perspective and influences
- Using Genres
	- template, recipe, conventions, mechanics but with own spin
	- find a problem with a genre and fix it
	- combining multiple genres
	- adding or taking away from a genre
	- swap out the metaphor of existing mechanics
	- understand genres core mechanics and build from there
- Making Mechanics
	- inspiration from real world experiences
	- inspiration from the way we control games
	- spin off of core mechanics
- The Experience
	- start from the experience and then pick the mechanics
	- inspiration is the fantasy
	- what role do you want the player to inhabit
	- start with a theme
	- personal experience or story

### General ideation tips

1. Break Games Down
	1. break down games into their smallest elements
	2. create a database of game mechanics and systems
2. Play Like a Critic
	1. identify pros and cons
	2. flaws may be opportunities for new games
3. Challenge Conventions
	1. take expectations of a game or genre and flip it on its head
4. Play All Games, Genres, and Mediums
	1. game ideas can come from the most unlikely places
5. Impose a Limitation
	1. give yourself some boundaries
6. Enter Game Jams
	1. themes are both a limitation and way to spark your imagination
7. Get in Your Editor
	1. sometimes [games design themselves](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kMDe7_YwVKI)
8. Listen to Music, Look at Art
	1. inspiration from visions, imagery, and feelings
9. Don't Think!
	1. don't always force ideas, let your mind wander
10. Keep it small
	1. think of a game idea as a seed - something that will grow throughout the development process

### Is this idea any good?

- Can you make it?
- Will this game stand out?
- How would you market it?
- Is the game idea actually fun?
